About Me!

Who I am

Born and raised in Bangladesh my early years were marked by a deep passion for the arts, particularly painting, which led me to successfully sell my works and engage with the local art community. I am also very interested in Video Games and have played Valorant competitively! From an early age, I was captivated by the intricate worlds and mechanics of video games, which led me to explore the underlying technologies that make these experiences possible. This fascination with gaming fueled my desire to understand and contribute to the development of innovative software and systems.

I have a cat names Luna who is technically my daughter and I lover her a lot :)

I am fluent in English, Bengali, Hindi, and Urdu, with partial proficiency in Arabic. In my free time I love making projects or hanging out with my friends going on side quests!

Educational Background

I'm currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at the University of South Florida, with an expected graduation in August 2026. My academic journey has been rewarding, with a cumulative GPA of 3.94 and a major GPA of 4.0.

My fascination with gaming fueled my desire to understand and contribute to the development of innovative software and systems. This is why I am pursuing a degree in computer science as it allows me to blend my enthusiasm for gaming with my technical skills, driving me to explore new advancements and create impactful solutions within the tech industry.

Fun Fact: I used to be an Architecture major and later switched to Computer Science!

Campus Involvement

I'm honored to be part of the Judy Genshaft Honors College and have received the USF Green and Gold Presidential Waiver Award. I've also made the Dean’s List for Fall 2023, Spring 2024, Fall 2024 and Spring 2025.

I currently serve as a Residential Assistant for USF’s Department of Residential Education, where I mentor a community of 30+ residents through conflict resolution, campus engagement, and professional development initiatives. I coordinate academic and social programs each semester, fostering an inclusive and growth-oriented environment that encourages students to thrive both personally and academically.

Previously, I served as the Student Co-ordinator for a year then the Vice President of the Bangladesh Student Association (BSA) for a year at USF. In the VP role, I led an executive team to organize 10+ cultural and educational events, connecting over 200 students across campus. I also collaborated with university administration and external partners to promote cross-cultural understanding and community engagement.
